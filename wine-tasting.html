Wine Tasting

I recently completed a wine tasting course at Cornell, which has deepened my appreciation for wine in both academic and everyday settings. Over the semester—and beyond the classroom—I’ve sampled a wide range of styles, from crisp whites to bold reds, and discovered nuances I never knew existed.

Below are three of my current favorites that really stood out:

2024 “Love You Bunches”, Stolpman Winery

Santa Barbara County, CA  |  Rosé Grenache 
  • Appearance: Pale salmon.
  • Aroma: Fresh strawberry, watermelon, and rose petal.
  • Palate: Bright acidity with red berry flavors and a hint of minerality.
  • Price: ~$28

2018 Chablis Premier Cru Vaillons

Domaine William Fèvre | Chablis, France  |  Chardonnay
  • Appearance: Light gold with green highlights.
  • Aroma: Green apple, citrus zest, and a sweaty‐stone note.
  • Palate: Crisp, steely minerality balanced by ripe pear and lemon.
  • Price: ~$60

2021 Blue Slate Riesling Kabinett

Dr. Loosen | Mosel, Germany  |  Riesling
  • Appearance: Pale straw.
  • Aroma: Slate, green apple, jasmine.
  • Palate: Light sweetness with zesty acidity and a vibrant finish.
  • Price: ~$25
